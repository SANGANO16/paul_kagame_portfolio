Biography of Paul Kagame

Early Life, Education, and Background

Quick Facts

  • Full Name: Paul Kagame
  • Born: Oct 23, 1957
  • Birthplace: Tambwe, Rwanda
  • Spouse: Jeannette Kagame

Introduction

Paul Kagame is the fourth and current President of Rwanda, serving since 2000. He is a former military officer and a key figure in the liberation of Rwanda and the end of the 1994 Genocide against the Tutsi.

Early Life

Born in 1957 in the southern province of Rwanda, Kagame’s family fled to Uganda when he was just two years old to escape ethnic violence. He spent his childhood in refugee camps, an experience that shaped his resilience and commitment to justice.

Education

Kagame attended primary school in the refugee camp and later moved to Ntare School in Mbarara, Uganda. He furthered his education in Kampala before receiving advanced military training at the US Army Command and General Staff College in Kansas, USA.

Career Beginnings

Kagame’s military career began in the late 1970s when he joined Yoweri Museveni’s rebel army in Uganda. He rose through the ranks to become a senior officer. In 1990, he took command of the Rwandan Patriotic Front (RPF) following the death of its initial leader, Fred Rwigyema.

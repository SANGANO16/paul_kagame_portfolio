April 7, 2026

32nd Commemoration of the Genocide against the Tutsi

Fellow Rwandans, Distinguished guests, Friends of Rwanda,

Today, we gather to mark the 32nd Commemoration of the Genocide against the Tutsi. This is a time to remember, to reflect, and to reaffirm our commitment to ensuring that what happened in 1994 never happens again—here or anywhere else in the world.

We honor the memory of more than one million innocent lives that were lost during the Genocide against the Tutsi. These were men, women, and children whose lives were cut short because of hatred, division, and the failure of humanity.

As a nation, we have chosen unity over division, resilience over despair, and hope over fear. Rwanda's journey since 1994 has not been easy, but it has been guided by the courage of our people and the determination to rebuild our country on the foundation of dignity and shared identity.

We must continue to confront the truth about our history. Remembering is not about dwelling in the past—it is about learning from it. It is about ensuring that future generations understand the consequences of hatred and the importance of protecting human life.

To the survivors, we stand with you. Your strength and resilience inspire us every day. Your stories remind us why we must remain vigilant against any form of genocide ideology and divisionism.

To the youth of Rwanda, you carry the responsibility of safeguarding our future. You must reject the seeds of hatred and embrace the values of unity, respect, and compassion.

The international community also has a role to play. The failure to act in 1994 must never be repeated. Genocide is preventable, and the world must always choose to act in defense of humanity.

As we commemorate today, let us renew our promise: Never Again.

Let us continue building a Rwanda that is united, peaceful, and prosperous—a country where every citizen is valued and where our shared humanity defines us.

I thank you.

Inauguration of New Eco-Friendly Housing Project

Fellow Rwandans, Distinguished guests, Partners and stakeholders,

Today marks an important milestone in our journey toward sustainable development as we inaugurate this new eco-friendly housing project.

This initiative reflects our shared vision of building a modern Rwanda that prioritizes environmental protection, efficient resource use, and improved quality of life for our citizens. It demonstrates that development and sustainability can go hand in hand.

As we confront the realities of climate change, projects like this are not a luxury—they are a necessity. By investing in green housing, we are reducing our environmental footprint while creating communities that are healthier, more resilient, and more inclusive.

This project is also a testament to the power of collaboration. Government institutions, private sector partners, and the community have come together to turn this vision into reality. Such partnerships are essential as we continue to transform our country.

Beyond providing homes, this development creates opportunities—jobs for our people, innovation for our industries, and a model that can be replicated across the country and beyond.

To the youth, I encourage you to embrace innovation and sustainability. The future belongs to those who can develop solutions that protect our environment while advancing our economy.

As we officially inaugurate this eco-friendly housing project, let us reaffirm our commitment to building a greener, more sustainable Rwanda for generations to come.

I thank you.

February 20, 2026

Keynote Address at the African Union Summit

Your Excellencies, Heads of State and Government, Chairperson of the African Union Commission, Distinguished delegates, Ladies and gentlemen,

It is an honor to address this esteemed gathering at the African Union Summit, where we come together to reflect on our shared progress and chart the path forward for our continent.

Africa stands at a critical moment in its history. We are a continent rich in potential, driven by a young and dynamic population, and endowed with vast natural and human resources. Yet, we must continue working together to transform this potential into tangible prosperity for all our people.

Regional integration remains central to our ambitions. Through initiatives such as the African Continental Free Trade Area, we have the opportunity to boost intra-African trade, strengthen our economies, and reduce dependency on external markets. It is now our responsibility to fully implement these frameworks and ensure that they deliver real benefits to our citizens.

Peace and security are the foundation of development. Without stability, our progress cannot be sustained. We must reinforce our collective mechanisms to prevent conflict, respond effectively to crises, and support nations in rebuilding stronger institutions.

Equally important is investment in our people. Education, healthcare, and technology must remain at the forefront of our priorities. Africa's youth are not just the leaders of tomorrow—they are the drivers of change today. We must equip them with the skills and opportunities needed to innovate and compete on a global scale.

Climate change continues to pose significant challenges to our continent. Africa contributes the least to global emissions, yet we bear a disproportionate burden of its impacts. We must advocate for climate justice while advancing sustainable solutions within our own countries.

Distinguished leaders, the future of Africa will be determined by the choices we make today. Let us strengthen our unity, uphold accountability, and remain committed to the vision of an integrated, prosperous, and peaceful continent.

Together, under the banner of the African Union, we can achieve the Africa we want.

I thank you.

Inauguration of Karama Model Village

Dear fellow Rwandans, Honored guests, Development partners,

Today, we gather to inaugurate Karama Model Village, a shining example of what can be achieved when we work together with determination and vision. This village represents more than just houses; it represents hope, dignity, and a better future for our people.

The Karama Model Village project was designed to provide modern, sustainable housing for families while creating a community that fosters social cohesion and economic opportunity. Each home is equipped with modern amenities, and the village includes schools, healthcare facilities, and commercial spaces.

This project demonstrates our commitment to ensuring that every Rwandan has access to decent housing. We believe that housing is not just a basic need but a foundation for human dignity and economic productivity.

The village also serves as a model for sustainable urban planning. With green spaces, renewable energy systems, and efficient waste management, Karama shows that development can be both modern and environmentally responsible.

To the residents of Karama, I say: this is your home, but it is also your responsibility. Take care of this community, work together with your neighbors, and make this village a place where children can grow up with hope and opportunity.

We will continue to replicate this model across Rwanda, ensuring that more families can benefit from similar developments. Our goal is to eliminate substandard housing and create communities where every Rwandan can thrive.

I extend my gratitude to all partners who contributed to this project, from government agencies to private sector partners and international supporters. Together, we are building the Rwanda we want.

I thank you.

Rwanda Launches New Digital Innovation Hub

Distinguished guests, Technology leaders, Young innovators,

Today marks another milestone in Rwanda's journey to become a knowledge-based economy. The launch of this Digital Innovation Hub represents our continued commitment to embracing technology as a driver of economic growth and social transformation.

This hub will serve as a center for innovation, providing young entrepreneurs and tech enthusiasts with the resources, mentorship, and networking opportunities they need to turn their ideas into successful businesses. From software development to artificial intelligence, from fintech to agritech, this hub will support innovations across all sectors.

We recognize that the future belongs to those who can harness the power of technology. That is why we have made significant investments in digital infrastructure, including nationwide fiber-optic connectivity and 4G coverage that reaches even the most remote parts of our country.

The Digital Innovation Hub is not just a building; it is an ecosystem. It brings together startups, established tech companies, investors, and academic institutions in a collaborative environment that fosters creativity and innovation.

To our young people, I say: the opportunities are here. Take advantage of this hub, develop your skills, and create solutions that address both local and global challenges. Rwanda needs your creativity, your energy, and your innovation.

This hub also strengthens Rwanda's position as a regional technology leader. We welcome partnerships with international tech companies and investors who see the potential in Africa's growing digital economy.

As we launch this hub, let us remember that technology is a means to an end. Our ultimate goal is to improve the lives of our people, create employment opportunities, and position Rwanda as a competitive player in the global digital economy.

I thank you.

December 10, 2025

Rwanda Hosts International Climate Summit

Your Excellencies, Distinguished delegates, Climate champions,

It is with great pride that Rwanda hosts this International Climate Summit, bringing together leaders, experts, and activists from around the world to address one of the most pressing challenges of our time.

Climate change is not a distant threat; it is a reality that affects millions of people today. In Africa, we feel its impacts acutely—droughts, floods, and changing weather patterns threaten our food security, our economies, and our way of life.

Yet, Africa is not just a victim of climate change. We are also part of the solution. Our continent has vast potential for renewable energy, from solar to wind to hydroelectric power. Our forests are vital carbon sinks that benefit the entire planet.

Rwanda is committed to doing our part. We have set ambitious targets for reducing our carbon emissions and increasing our use of renewable energy. Our ban on single-use plastics and our reforestation efforts are just the beginning.

But we cannot do this alone. The international community must honor its commitments to climate financing, particularly for developing countries that are most vulnerable to climate impacts yet have contributed the least to the problem.

This summit is an opportunity to strengthen partnerships, share knowledge, and commit to concrete actions. Let us leave this gathering with clear plans and renewed determination to protect our planet for future generations.

The choices we make today will determine the world we leave to our children. Let us choose wisely, let us choose sustainability, and let us choose action.

I thank you.

New Year Address to the Nation

Dear fellow Rwandans, Compatriots at home and abroad,

As we welcome the new year, I extend my warmest wishes to every Rwandan family. The year behind us was filled with both challenges and achievements, and as we look ahead, we do so with confidence and determination.

Our economy continues to grow, our people are healthier and better educated, and our nation stands tall among the community of nations. These achievements belong to all of us—they are the result of your hard work, your resilience, and your commitment to building a better Rwanda.

In the coming year, we will continue to focus on economic transformation, ensuring that growth translates into better lives for all our citizens. We will invest in infrastructure, expand access to quality education and healthcare, and create more opportunities for our young people.

We will also continue to strengthen our institutions, fight corruption, and ensure that every Rwandan has a voice in our democracy. Our governance model is built on accountability, and we will not waver in our commitment to transparency and the rule of law.

To our youth, you are the future of this nation. Continue to work hard, embrace innovation, and never lose sight of the values that define us as Rwandans—unity, integrity, and excellence.

To our diaspora, you remain an integral part of our national family. Your contributions, both financial and intellectual, continue to support our development. We encourage you to stay connected and to participate in our nation's growth.

As we step into this new year, let us do so with hope and determination. Together, there is nothing we cannot achieve.

Happy New Year, Rwanda!

I thank you.

National Umuganda: Building Communities Together

Dear fellow Rwandans,

Today, as we do on the last Saturday of every month, we come together for Umuganda—our national community work day. This tradition is more than just cleaning and building; it is a powerful expression of our unity and collective responsibility.

Umuganda embodies the spirit of Ubumwe—unity—that has been the foundation of Rwanda's recovery and development. When we work together, we accomplish what no individual could achieve alone. We build schools, repair roads, plant trees, and strengthen the bonds that hold our communities together.

This month's Umuganda focuses on environmental protection, as we prepare for the rainy season. We are planting trees, cleaning waterways, and building terraces to prevent soil erosion. These activities protect our environment and ensure sustainable agriculture for our farmers.

I want to thank every Rwandan who participates in Umuganda. Your dedication to community service sets an example for the world. In Rwanda, we do not wait for government to solve every problem—we roll up our sleeves and get to work.

To our international friends who have joined us today, welcome. Umuganda is open to everyone who wants to experience the spirit of Rwandan unity. We hope you will take this spirit back to your own communities.

As we work together today, let us remember that every tree we plant, every road we repair, and every community project we complete brings us closer to the Rwanda we want—a nation of prosperity, sustainability, and shared success.

Let us continue to build, together.

I thank you.

November 15, 2025

Launch of Universal Health Coverage Expansion

Distinguished guests, Healthcare professionals, Fellow Rwandans,

Today, we mark a significant milestone in our journey toward ensuring that every Rwandan has access to quality healthcare. The expansion of our Universal Health Coverage program will bring comprehensive healthcare services to even the most remote communities in our country.

Healthcare is a fundamental human right, not a privilege. When we invest in the health of our people, we invest in the future of our nation. Healthy citizens are productive citizens, and a healthy nation is a prosperous nation.

Our Mutuelle de Santé program has been a remarkable success, covering over 90% of our population. This expansion will close the remaining gaps, ensuring that no Rwandan is left behind when it comes to accessing medical care.

The expansion includes new health centers, additional medical personnel, and enhanced services including maternal care, pediatric services, and chronic disease management. We are also investing in telemedicine to connect remote communities with specialists in our major hospitals.

To our healthcare workers, you are the backbone of this system. Your dedication, especially during the recent global health challenges, has been extraordinary. We will continue to invest in your training, your equipment, and your working conditions.

This expansion is made possible by our strong partnership with development partners who share our vision of health for all. We extend our gratitude to all who have contributed to this effort.

As we launch this expansion, let us remember that healthcare is a shared responsibility. Citizens must take care of their health through preventive measures, and communities must support health initiatives in their areas.

Together, we will build a healthier Rwanda.

I thank you.
